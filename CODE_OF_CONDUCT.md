# Code of Conduct

Thermalith is a small, friendly open-source project. The short version: be decent to each other.

That covers almost everything, but a few specifics help.

## What we're going for

- Assume good faith. Most friction is a misunderstanding, not malice.
- Be patient and welcoming, especially with beginners and first-time contributors. Everyone was new once.
- Keep criticism about the code, not the person. "This breaks on Linux because X" is useful; "you clearly didn't test this" is not.
- Disagree freely, but do it respectfully. Strong opinions are fine; contempt isn't.

## Not welcome

Harassment, personal attacks, discrimination or slurs of any kind, sexualized comments or imagery, deliberate intimidation, sustained disruption, or publishing someone's private information. None of that belongs here.

This applies anywhere the project lives: issues, pull requests, commit messages, discussions, and any other project space.

## If something goes wrong

Email **evilgenius@evilgeniuslabs.ca**. Reports are read by the maintainer and handled privately. Depending on what happened, that might mean a quiet word, removing a comment, or banning someone from the project. We'll use judgment and aim to be fair.

The maintainer has the final say on what crosses the line, and the goal is always the same: keep this a place people are glad to contribute to.
